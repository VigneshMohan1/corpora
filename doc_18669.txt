Bihar, located in eastern India, has been popular as the birthplace of Buddhism over the years. The state is considered a great center of learning, power and culture. Nalanada and Vikramshila were two of the important seats of learning in ancient Bihar. Bihar has also been home to many powerful dynasties, including the Mauryan Empire. The population of Bihar is 103,804,637 (2011 census) and covers an area of 94,163 km sq. Districts and Administration of Bihar: Bihar is divided into 38 districts and 9 divisions. The head of bureaucracy in the state is the Chief Secretary and under this position are officers from the Indian Administrative Services and the Indian Police Service. The Chief Justice heads the judiciary in the state.
