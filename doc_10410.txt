Go to the shop Jain Irrigation Announces Fund Raising Plans Home Jain Irrigation Announces Fund Raising Plans Jalgaon, Maharashtra-based Jain Irrigation plans to raise funds to the tune of $200 mn to strengthen its balance sheet, reduce interest cost, and balance its currency exposure with long term maturities. The Securities Issuance Committee of the Board of Directors of Jain Irrigation Systems Limited has decided to raise long term funds by way of: a) Issue of equity shares on preferential issue for approx. $73 mn at a price Rs.80 per share or the issue price worked out in accordance with the applicable provisions of the SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2009 (Regulation 76), whichever is higher. b) Issue of Foreign Currency Convertible Bonds (FCCBs) to raise approx. $55 mn, the terms of which are: (i) Conversion at Rs.115 per equity share at any time during the period of 5 years. (ii) The FCCB shall have a coupon of 3% p.a. and yield to maturity of 6%. (iii) The FCCBs shall not be listed on any International Stock Exchanges, but the resultant equity shares shall be listed on the Indian Stock Exchanges. (iv) The FCCB’s will be repaid in one tranche after 5 years, if not already converted into equity shares. c) Raising of LIBOR linked External Commercial Borrowings (ECB) up to an amount of $75 mn. The ECB shall have a tenor of 6-10 years. d) The Securities Issuance Committee (SIC) approved raising of approx. Rs.647.25 mn by way of equity warrants to promoter group on the 27th August 2012 preferential issue under the SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2009. The company plans to convene an extraordinary general meeting to seek approval of the shareholders for the above equity and FCCB issuances and the related issues. Mr Anil B Jain, Managing Director of the Company said, “We have been able to put together significant resource raising from quality investors like International Finance Corporation (IFC), lenders like Rabo bank, and others during these volatile environment and uncertain market conditions.” Jain Irrigation Systems Ltd.,(JISL), specializes in drip irrigation. The major portion of the business relates to micro irrigation systems and plastic pipes. Other business interests include food processing, onion dehydration, tissue culture, and solar appliances. It clocks an annual turnover of close to $1 billion. For more information, contact : Jain Plastic Park, N.H.No. 6, Bambhori, Jalgaon – 425001. India.
