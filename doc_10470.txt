Based at Pune Sanjay Deshmukh Mr. Sanjay Deshmukh is the Managing Director of Natural Organic certification Agro (NOCA), established in 2004 in Pune. NOCA have accredited certification body under APEDA from Government of India Ministry of Commerce. After receiving their first accreditation in the year 2005, NOCA started certification programs. They have collaboration with Italian company called Bio Agri Cert. NOCA’s certification program is based on the ISO signified standard. Mr. Deshmukh is a post graduate in Horticulture, also served as professor in Agriculture University, Maharashtra. Achievement : “We had started this company with the domestic certification in the year 2005. We used to do the certification program only for the Indian public and they had started the Organic certification program for export. We carry out certification as per the SUOP, National Program for Organic Production (NOP), and EC standards.” says Mr. Deshmukh. NOCA’s annual turnover is around 3.5 crores. “Our certification is accepted worldwide that is because of our name and our continuous presence in the international exhibitions and our client based outside India”, he adds. NOCA was started with the aim to promote organic agriculture in Maharashtra and other parts of India. In the beginning of their company they were indulged only in creating awareness program in organic agriculture, formation of farmers group, do their certification as per the Indian standards and guide them etc. They are associated with Maharashtra Organic Farmers Federation, which in turn is a member of OFAI and ICCOA. Challenges : “In India most of the farmers go to a contractor who gets certification for them. So there is a need to do this certification programme for them also,” says Mr. Deshmukh. Organic Certification for Farmers :  NOCA is working towards making organic certification procedure easier for farmers and the TDS documentation also being made simpler so that the maximum information can be obtained from the farmer. On the basis of evaluation of those information farmer shall be given authenticity certification in organics. The entire organic certification program is based on 15 week starts from the conversion day. Mr. Deshmukh informs farmer cannot be certified as organic farmer in a day. It is a process with certain rules and fixed period which needs to be followed. Once farmers go through the certification procedure and pass conversion periods then only organic certification is given. He concludes, “if a person want to grow the organic market there should be an authenticity of the organic product and we are working very hard to give the authenticity for the organic products through our certification program.” says Mr. Deshmukh.
