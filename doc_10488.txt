Go to the shop Osaw Agro Industries – Supplier of Cleaning and grading machines Home Osaw Agro Industries – Supplier of Cleaning and grading machines Manufactures and supplies sorting, cleaning and grading machines Osaw Agro Industries was founded in 1984. It is one of the leading manufacturers of machines meant for cleaning, grading and sorting seeds, all cereals, spices, pulses and oilseeds. The company also manufactures sorting, cleaning and grading machines of all fruits and vegetables. In the seed industry it provides world class seed grading equipment to seed producers at affordable prices. Its customers are also educated about subsidy schemes to promote seed industry in the country. It is located at Ambala in Haryana. Last Osaw clocked the annual turnover of Rs. 55 Crores. The company has collaborations with companies in various countries. These include Damas in Denmark, NoroGard in Sweden, Araj in Poland, Technofruita in Spain, Kongsklide in Denmark and BMC Engineering in New Zealand. “Our equipments are used in the seed, grain, fruits and vegetables industry. We contribute in the development of these sectors” shares Mr. Sanjeev Sagar, Managing Director. Besides seed and grain industry Osaw is doing well in the horticulture field. Owing to increase in awareness in the field it has diversified into machines meant for washing, sorting, grading and sorting of the same. The company has technical collaborations with Tecnofrita of Spain for supply of electronic sorting, grading line in India and BMC Engineering of New Zealand for mechanical lines. “We have CNC turret punch press, NC press brake, NC shearing machine, welding table, router, planner, paint plant etc” says Mr. Sanjeev while throwing light on the various machineries that the company uses for its services. The company places equal importance to after sales services as much as it does to its products. It offers annual maintenance contract scheme for the customers ensuring the year round service of its machinery and equipment. Mr. Sagar opines the only challenge the Indian seed industry faces today is the low replacement rate as compared to developed country. However he foresee a change as growth and a big improvement in the replacement rate in coming years. He adds, “hybrid seeds and plant biotechnology is playing vital role in high yield production. With high production we can feed the growing population”. A lot of projects for seed, grains and fruits and vegetables in India and abroad are currently being taken up by Osaw. In the future, the company plans to upgrade technology for automation and covering wider range of postharvest activities. Mr. Sanjeev Sagar has more than 30 years of experience in this field.
