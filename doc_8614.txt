May 2015 Climate Change Resilience in Asia's Cities By 2025, 21 out of 37 of the world's megacities - cities with 10 million or more people - will likely be from the Asia and Pacific region. How can the region cope with this rapid urbanization while maintaining environmental sustainability? May 2015 Building Climate Change Resilience for Asia's Urban Poor A new fund that targets climate change resilience in 25 Asian Cities will be provided by an innovative partnership between ADB, the UK Department for International Development (DFID) Foundation and Rockefeller Foundation. The goal is to leverage and scale up solutions to protect some of the world's most vulnerable urban communities. April 2015 迈向2020：亚行十点行动计划 为实现消除亚太地区贫困的愿景，亚行将向最需要投资的领域投入更多资源。 April 2015 日新月异的亚太地区—亚行也在变 亚行承诺对成员体不断变化的发展需求保持相关性和相应性。通过《2020战略》中期检查（MTR）、亚行长期战略日程以及MTR行动计划，我们进一步明确了业务重点，即更好地应对地区发展挑战。本年报总结了2014年亚行的业绩。 April 2015 Toward 2020: ADB's Ten-Point Action Plan To deliver our vision of an Asia and Pacific region free from poverty, ADB will channel more resources into areas where they’re needed most. April 2015 Asia and the Pacific Is Changing Fast and So Is ADB ADB is committed to remaining relevant and responsive to the evolving needs of our member countries. Through the Strategy 2020 MTR and the MTR Action Plan, we have sharpened our operational focus to better address the region's development challenges. April 2015 Recent Significant Disasters in the Asia and Pacific Region The recent disasters that affected Nepal and the Philippines serve as a call to risk-prone countries throughout Asia and the Pacific to build and vigilantly maintain resilience to catastrophes. March 2015 Asian Development Outlook 2015 - Growth Outlook Developing Asia’s growth will be steady, supported by a strengthening recovery in the major industrial economies and soft global commodity prices. March 2015 Migration and Remittances in Asia and the Pacific A remittance is a transfer of money by a foreign worker to an individual in his/her home country. Workers’ remittances are a significant part of international capital flows, especially in the case of labour-exporting countries.
