The programme was taken up on a long term sustainable approach of three years with the budget allocation of Rs.35.00 lakhs for 2010-11. As against this the Corporation has incurred an expenditure of Rs.29.66 lakhs. During the Year 2011-12, Corporation has incurred an expenditure of Rs.36.45 against the budget allocation of Rs.40.00 lakhs. Participation of SC/ST/Women components are as follows: - Maharashtra Tamil Nadu Orissa Total farmers 760 836 460 SC/ST 148 85  123 Women 86 428 87 Others 526 323 250 Free Health Check up programme for cotton farmers: Cotton farmers, being one of the important factors in cotton sector, the Corporation as per the MoU and as a part of CSR activity has organized free Health Check-programme for the cotton farmers of backward areas lacking basic infrastructural medical facilities particularly in Andhra Pradesh and Maharashtra. The programmes were conducted with the consultation of Medical Officer of concerned Public Health Centre. Free medical services included such as gynecology, pediatrician, ENT, ophthalmology, orthopedic and general medicines. Besides consultation, prescribe medicines have been distributed amongst the participating farmers and their family members. In Maharashtra, the Health Check up programme was conducted in Jalgaon district at Goradkheda in Pachora taluka on 14th October 2012 with 509 beneficiary farmers, at Naigaon in Nanded district on 17th October 2012 with 342 beneficiaries; at Khanepuri in Jalna district on 29th November 2012 with 442 beneficiaries and at Gevarai in Gevrai district on 30th November 2012 with 325 beneficiaries. The Health Check up programme was conducted in Warangal district at Medram village of Tadwai mandal on 4th November 2012 wherein 500 farmers/their family members were the beneficiaries. Thus, total 2013 cotton farmers/their family members have been benefitted through the free health check up programme of the Corporation under CSR. Corporate Social Responsibility & Responsiveness is being implemented in CCI under the guidance of Board level sub-committee and is coordinated by Group of officials (headed by an executive not one rank below the board level).
