Go to the shop Indian Floriculture Home Indian Floriculture Growing over 15% annually International Flora Expo between 3-5, March 2012 India is positioned as one of the world’s largest and fastest growing retail markets.   Modern retail in India could be worth US$ 175-200 billion by 2016.  With the economy booming, competition in the market place is fierce.  Rising income, increasing consumerism in urban areas and an upswing in rural consumption will fuel this growth to around 7-8 per cent. Floriculture is taking strong roots in India, which is emerging as a reliable floriculture supplier to the world. Keeping in view the paramount importance of promotion of floriculture development in the country, and recognizing the importance of the sector’s contribution to national agricultural economy, the Government of India has introduced many developmental programmes mainly through  the schemes of Ministry of Agriculture (National Horticulture Board, National Horticulture Mission, Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States etc.) and Ministry of Commerce (APEDA). Most of the state governments have also initiated their own programmes providing technical and financial assistance to millions of small and large producers. With over 300 million middle and higher income population, India is the world’s second largest consumer base and fastest growing retail destination. Flower consumption, growing at a whopping 30% per annum and numerous festivals, along with increasing modernization and per capita imcome, make India a floral super power of future. A huge domestic market supports high quality export oriented flower production by providing a unique competitive edge. India, China, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka and Pakistan along with other countries of the region make South Asia, the world’s largest market and India is emerging as world’s fastest growing flower and gardening market. In all major, second and third tier cities flower retail shops have mushroomed all over the place in the form of modern retail outlets, roadside shops and also wholesale bouquet making companies supplying to corporate buyers.  Now a new era is going to open with modern retailing concepts.  India also became world’s attractive tourism destination creating new boom to consumption of flowers and plants industry. India, has unique culture of flower consumption in many forms like garlands, flower-carpets, floral rains, florial jewelry, to name a few. Flora Expo 2012 will bring you not only conventional flower and gardening industry professionals but also mass buyers from new types of large industry retailers such as home centers, supermarkets, departmental stores and interior shops. Ministry of Agriculture and its departments are targeting to increase the production and the focus is on attracting more and more investments of Rs. 10,000 crore in the next few years. The business opportunity in Indian floriculture that can simultaneously create a million jobs in the rural areas. Surely, many factors are conducive for the growth of this industry to meet the growing needs of both domestic and export markets. To upgrade the industry, India has emerged as one of the largest importers of new varieties from International breeders, planting materials, greenhouses, cold chain machineries, irrigation fertigation products, florist & floral art accessories, fertilizers and packaging solutions etc from The Netherlands, Germany, France, Spain, Taiwan and China. New challenges are emerging for India’s growing floriculture industry. Even as the domestic demand for flowers is growing annually by 15-20 per cent in major cities, the export growth has struck a bumpy path. It is therefore appropriate that to give fillip to floriculture sector, the 7th International Flora Expo 2012 to be held from 3 – 5 March 2012 at NSIC Exhibition Complex, Okhla, New Delhi, India. This largest floriculture extravaganza has been conceptualized by Media Today Group, India’s leading agro based publication chain and event Management Company along with iFlora (Indian Flowers and Ornamental Plants Welfare Association), a non-profit promotion body. Mr S. Jafar Naqvi said that India is emerging a new production centre has many advantages as availability of labour, good water and soils and ample sunlight and a very strong domestic market as a backbone for the floriculture industry. Given the proper incentives, the farmers there are ready to diversify their cropping patterns towards floriculture and thus sustain the growth of this sector.
