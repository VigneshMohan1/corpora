Go to the shop Dr. Y S Parmar University of Horticulture and Forestry Home Dr. Y S Parmar University of Horticulture and Forestry Offers need based and farmer-oriented programs in horticulture and forestry Dr. Vijay Singh Thakur Dr. Y S Parmar University of Horticulture and Forestry is located in Nauni, Solan. The university offers a range of research programs to bring about a change in agriculture. It is determined to impart technical knowhow to end-users for overall development of the rural masses. The university aims at strategic, need based and farmer-oriented development of horticulture and forestry in Himachal Pradesh. Improved livelihood opportunities and economic prosperity of farming communities drive the university’s activities. Of the varied research programs being carried out in the university, a few are listed below: • Development of strategies for harnessing full horticultural potential of traditional fruit growing areas • Expansion of fruit culture in non-traditional areas with full technological support • Ensured supply of superior planting material • Improvement in productivity and quality of fruit crops • Micro propagation of superior genotypes of important fruit crops • Gene tagging, gene mining, identification and isolation of novel genes • Bioinformatics silicon sequence analysis of important horticulture plants The total strength of the students at the university is 1333 with 598 boys and 735 girls. Some of the courses offered by the university include: • M.Sc. Horticulture • M. Sc. Food Technology • M.Sc. (Ag.) Seed Science & Technology • M.Sc. Forestry (Agroforestry, Environmental Management, Forest Genetic Resources, Wood Science & Technology, Medicinal & Aromatic Plants, Plantation Technology, Silviculture, Watershed Management) • M.Sc. (Ag.) Soil Science • M.Sc. (Ag.) Agricultural Economics • M.Sc. (Ag.) Entomology, Nematology, Plant Pathology • M.Sc. Molecular Biology & Biotechnology, Microbiology • M.Sc. Environmental Science • M.Sc. Statistics The Experiential Learning Program (ELP) was started from the Academic session 2011-12 for B.Sc Horticulture final year students on the concept of earn while you learn. 60 B.Sc (Hons) students are currently undergoing training under this program. The students of the university are trained in quality planting material production of horticultural crops, seeds of vegetable and flower crops as well as, medicinal and aromatic plants, value addition in fruits and vegetables, fruits and vegetable preservation, bakery and confectionery products, health foods, post-harvest management, food analysis and quality control management, food safety and testing. Students take their internships at various regional research stations spread across different agro-ecological zones of the state. “The main purpose of the internships is to get the students acquainted with different zones of the state and farmers growing different horticultural crops,” shares Dr. Vijay Singh Thakur, Vice Chancellor. The university hopes to continue imparting quality education, proper environment and infrastructure to the students at undergraduate and postgraduate levels. It plans to have more seminar halls or at least one in each department. Besides equipping the laboratories, updating the library and computer aided education in each department is also envisaged. Plans of International human resource exchange in education and research are also on the cards. Field laboratories shall be constructed at the experimental farms of each department and will be equipped with all the teaching aid and research facilities.
