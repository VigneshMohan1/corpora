Go to the shop Bedico Automotives – spare parts of harvester combine and rotavators Home Bedico Automotives – spare parts of harvester combine and rotavators Ludhiana based manufacturer and supplier of spare parts of harvester combine and rotavators Sh. Kuldip Chand Bedi Bedico Automotives came into existence in year 2000. Late Sh. Kuldip Chand Bedi is the founder. Initially the company located at Ludhiana was manufacturing motorcycle spare parts but later switched to spare parts for harvest combines and rotavators. Annual turnover is Rs. 12 Crores per annum. Products Company manufactures augar/worms (for harvester combines and tractors), blades (for rotavator and power tiller), chains (for harvester combines, rotavator, reaper, thrasher, power tiller) and oil gear pumps – with bearing support (for harvester combines). Mr. Anil Bedi, Chairman of Bedico Automotives says, “all our products are designed and manufactured after indepth research and most appropriate raw material in alliance with Indian soil of different states.” He further adds, “our harvester chains are having long lifespan. Rotavator blades are designed to suit every kind of Indian soil and PTO shafts sustains water logging paddy fields.” Challenges Farmers, the target customers are largely depended on Government subsidy to buy agriculture implements and equipments to bring in mechanization in their farms. In states where subsidy is provided farmers are able to make the purchase however in few states where such subsidies are not available farmers are not able to buy inspite of having immediate need for the same. Sales Market Mr. Bedi shares, “we have divided market into two, first 60-70% sale comes from the original product manufacturer like rotavator manufacture or harvester combine manufacture. Second comes after margin, this year we are selling across India and targeting the dealers and good customers. We have well planned sales network in India monitored by 5 sales managers. “ Product Awareness Website and trade fairs are the two prime modes to create product awareness. Further advertisements in different agriculture related magazines and channels are also helpful in reaching customers. Farm Mechanization Mr. Bedi opines, “Indian farmers need new technology and mechanization for food processing rather than farming which shall help them attaining higher profits. In farming Indian farmers are using adequate farming technology like drip irrigation, soil preparation tools, harvesting techniques etc.’ Future Plans In coming years company shall be focusing on sales expansion in North and Eastern states of India. Mr. Bedi says, “mechanization and technology enhancement in agriculture depends substantially on Government initiative. If Government supports farmers then we as agricultural equipments’ providers surely shall provide best solutions.” Mr. Anil Bedi did his graduation from Government college Ludhiana, followed by MBA (marketing) in 1992. He has wide engineering experience which he credits to his practical work experience involving interactions with customer, dealers, shop floor mechanics, higher management of different organizations and participation in foreign exhibitions.
