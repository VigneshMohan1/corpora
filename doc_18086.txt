HIV in India India, with more than 1 billion residents, is considered to be one the most thickly populated countries in the world. Amongst them, about 2.4 million are presently suffering from Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). Though as per the Aids Report 2011 published by the United Nations, new HIV infectivity in India has declined at the rate of 50 % during the past 10 years. HIV Prevalence Rate in India In spite of having world's third biggest inhabitants suffering from the disease, the HIV prevalence rate in India is less than numerous other countries. During 1990s, HIV infection rates rose like an epidemic affecting every Indian society irrespective of the fact whether they are truck drivers or sex workers, who are said to be the original carrier of the infection. Being a populated country of over a billion, a rise of 0.1 % adds more than half a million HIV patients. Survey shows about 2.4 million people were affected by the HIV in India in the year 2009. Among this, the HIV prevalence rate in adults was estimated as 0.31 %. This rate declined from 0.36 % as it was in the year 2006. The rate of HIV infection among youngsters aging between 15 to 24 years has even shown a declining trend. State and group-wise distribution of HIV Prevalence Rate in India The north-eastern and southern part of India has got high HIV prevalence rate. The major states contributing to the overall HIV prevalence rate in India are: Maharashtra: 0.55 % Karnataka: 0.63 % Nagaland: 0.78 % Mizoram: 0.81 % Andhra Pradesh: 0.90 % Manipur: 1.40 % Till the year 2007, the HIV prevalence rate among different groups are: Women sex workers: 5.1 % Homosexual Men: 7.4 % Females going to Antenatal clinics: 0.48 % People going to Sexually Transmitted Disease (STD) clinics: 3.6 % Drug users: 7.2 % HIV Prevalence Rate These groups again vary from state to state.
