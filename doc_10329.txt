Nagaraj – Program Coordinator, KVK Ramnagara (Karnataka) Promotes farming system that leads to constant and higher income for farmers “With the objective of getting constant income throughout the year, KVK, Ramnagara, Bangalore started promoting integrated farming system,” says Dr. K.H. Nagaraj, Program Coordinator. He adds, “The objective is effectively integrating different enterprises wherein the output of one enterprise would be the input for the other enterprise with symbiotic relationship.” Under this program the organization is working on synergistic interaction and optimum utilization of available resources in the farm and suggesting new technology on mechanization and value addition. Integrated farming module enables for higher earnings from a unit area. Different IFS modules can be applied depending on the land size and the resources available. A typical model promoted by KVK is of 2.5 acres. “Profit earned under IFS model under different enterprises varies but one can earn up to Rs. 6.6 lakhs from 2.5 acres,” assures Dr. Nagaraj. An illustration of the earning in one hectare of farm land as shared by Dr Nagaraj- Areca nut 10 tonnes, cost of cultivation would be Rs 35,000 with gross profit of Rs 1,95,000 resulting in net profit of Rs 1,60,000; Vegetables’ input cost is Rs 38,000 and net profit is Rs.2,07,000, for planting 1200 rose plants cost is Rs 30,000 and net profit is Rs. 1,80,000; Cost incurred for growing marigold 1500 plants is Rs. 8,000 and the net profit is Rs 47,000; Flock of 16 sheep costs costs Rs. 60,000 and net profit is Rs. 68,000 and lastly vermicompost of 1-1.5 tonnes would give a net profit of Rs. 1700 with input cost being Rs. 500. Here Rose, Marigold and vegetables are inter-crops. This illustration shows a total earning of Rs. 6,63,700. “Integrated farming model requires dedication, hard work and timely implementation of perfect crop planning, marketing information and timely adoption of new scientific technology,” shares Dr. Nagaraj. Skills required of farmers would be timely cultural operations, commitment and dedication of all family members and effective utilization of on-farm resources. More advanced skill required would be timely implementation of scientific technology and proper crop planning based on market demand and supply information. Clarifying the help provided by KVK, Dr. Nagaraj points out that it offers only technical assistance and no monetary assistance. “Many farmers are now open to the new IFS model and few have already adopted it at their farms,” shares Dr. Nagaraj, “Department officials too have visited and have been involved in promoting the same model at their jurisdictions.” Till date a total of 10 farmers have adopted the typical model.
