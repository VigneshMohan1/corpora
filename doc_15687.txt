Situated in Punjab Amritsar is one of the most frequented cities in India. The city which is home to the famous Harmandir Saheb or Golden Temple is a one stop destination for most Sikh tourists to India. It is believed that this holy shrine experiences more tourists than the Taj Mahal in Agra. Most of the tourists that visit Delhi also visit this popular city. There are several trains and buses that operate from Delhi to Amritsar. The journey from Delhi to Amritsar by road is very pleasant as you come across several beautiful fields and landscapes. Travelling from Delhi to Amritsar Flights from Amritsar to Delhi Amritsar shares a very strong air network with Delhi. Most of the international and domestic airlines connect both these cities. The Amritsar airport known as Raja Sansi International Airport is situated at a distance of 11 Km from the city center. Flights like Jet Airways, Kingfisher and Air India operate between the two cities almost on a daily basis. It generally takes almost an hour to reach Amritsar from Delhi by flight. Trains from Amritsar to Delhi Since Amritsar is one of the important cities in Punjab from the commercial point of view it is but obvious that the city will be connected to the rest of the country and specially Delhi via a good rail network. There are several trains that run between Amritsar and Delhi almost every day. The journey by train from Amritsar to Delhi takes almost 7. 30 hours and most trains that leave Delhi reach the Amritsar Junction which is the main railway station in the city. Road Transport from Amritsar to Delhi Traveling from Delhi to Amritsar by road is also a very good option since both the cities are connected with a decent highway network. Since Amritsar is situated at a distance of 465 Km from Delhi the journey is a little long but a fun packed one. The journey is extremely scenic and smooth and most of the state buses ply between the two cities on a regular basis.
