Go to the shop Anantharaman – Promotes modernization in farming Home Anantharaman – Promotes modernization in farming Mumbai based consultant, promotes modernization in farming Mr. Anantharaman is a consultant hailing from an agricultural family. He started his  venture a year back with coconut farm of 7 acres in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu. Also, looking for a barren land to cultivate the same with latest technology and wishes to explore solar power for cultivation as electricity is a major concern in Tamil Nadu. “I have also planned to grow spices, pulses, pineapple in between the coconut trees as they are more than 15 to 20 years old,” shares Mr. Anantharaman. He makes around Rs. 50,000 a month from coconuts alone. Presently based at Mumbai, Mr. Anantharaman shall be soon exploring other modern techniques on soil in his farm. “I wish to enrich the soil by invoking the vermi compost. This can give me better results. And I also want to start a dairy farm at the earliest,” he adds. Hailing from Maharashtra he shares the status of agriculture in Maharashtra. “Maharashtra has come a hard way and they have lots of institutions to help the farmers with utmost. Moreover they have plenty of water in certain parts of Maharashtra,” he says. Some farmers cultivate up to 125 tonnes of sugarcane in an acre, where size is around 22 feet. Guava and Pomegranate are other highly rich cultivation in the state, all of which are exported, he adds. Indian Farmers : It is essential to have help centres across to help farmers gain knowledge and help on cultivation as per fertility of soil, opines Mr. Anantharaman. In India, modernization in agriculture is crucial. Secondly, farmers must be provided or they should seek assistance in marketing of their finished products. This should be made easy for them and the security for the sale of goods must be provided. Future Plans : He plans to buy more land in the future and continue using solar energy and better seed management. Also interested to explore the organic side of farming and negate chemicals completely. He appreciates water and electricity management. Plans to modernize agricultural sector and educate farmers to uplift them, as well as develop the sector. Besides, he believes hassles of selling the finished products should be made easier for the farmers. Mr. Anantharaman is a B.A. in Statistics with diploma in computer programming and materials management. He is well versed with legal aspects of property buying and selling.
