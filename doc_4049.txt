We expect graduates of our program to effectively: Apply the mathematical, physical, engineering, and biological principles needed to understand, analyze, and solve problems in food, agricultural, environmental, and/or biological systems; Communicate via oral, written, and visual methods; Accomplish tasks, as an individual and contributor, to multi-disciplinary teams; Understand the social, environmental, safety, and economic impacts of their work in local and global contexts, and to perform in a professional and ethical manner; and Be committed to enhancing knowledge and skills through continuing education. A quick look at Biosystems and Agricultural Engineering at Oklahoma State University Enrollment:  140 Students Student Organizations:  Alpha Epsilon, American Society of Agricultural and Biological Engineers, Cowboy Motorsports, Fountain Wars Award Winning Students:  International award winning Cowboy Motorsports team; 2007 Truman Scholar; 2007, 2008 and 2011 Udall Scholars; NSF Graduate Fellowships (to see our student awards, please view our  current  and  archived news  pages) Curriculum Sample:  Students majoring in biosystems and agricultural engineering take classes in: • Heat and mass transfer; • Engineering design; • Experimental methods; • Physical properties of biological materials; • Natural resources engineering; and • Instruments and controls.
