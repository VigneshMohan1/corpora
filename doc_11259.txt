Go to the shop Tamil Nadu Organic Certification Home Tamil Nadu Organic Certification The Tamil Nadu Department of Organic Certification (TNOCD) was set up by government of Tamil Nadu in 2007. The Department carries out inspection and certification of organic production system in accordance with National Program for Organic Production (NPOP) norms. Mr. T. Gopalakrishnan is the director; Mr. S. Krishnaraj is the quality manager; and Dr. Tom. P. Silas is the evaluator of TNOCD. TNOCD certifies cereals, pulses, oil seeds, cotton, sugarcane, horticultural, medicinal and forest crop. It also approves organic inputs for use in organic agriculture. Trade and export approval are also given to operators for trading on organic products. TNOCD has 28114 acres registered for certification with a participation of around 9000 farmers. Process Involved : After an application is filed, an organic certification inspector of TNOCD carries out onsite inspection. If the operator complies with all requirements, the certificate is issued. Individual in the categories of small and large farmers, corporate, and groups having a minimum of 25 to a maximum of 500 members with legal authority can apply for certification. Fees : A registration fee of Rs.500 is collected from small and marginal farmers. Other farmers are charged Rs.1000 along with inspection charges of Rs.1000 and travel charges of Rs.200. For farmers groups the registration fee is Rs.5000 along with Rs.1000 for inspection charges and Rs.200 for travel. For corporate/business registration fee is Rs.5000 along with Rs.2000 for inspection and Rs.400 for travel. The fee for Scope Certificate and Transaction Certificate is Rs.1000 and Rs.500, respectively.
