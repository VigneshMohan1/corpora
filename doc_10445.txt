Annual turnover Rs. 15 crores Prateek Vaish Model Dairy Farm was established in the year 1979 at Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh by Dr. S.K.Vaish. Along with activities of milk production, cattle breeding, processing of milk into value added products and cattle trading, the organization conceptualized to provide consulting and advisory services in all aspects of dairy farming. It also has its own dairy with more than three hundred cattle. The turnover of the company is projected at Rs. 15 crores. Mr. Prateek Vaish, VP Operations highlights major achievements of Model Dairy Farm as amongst the first few dairy farms to start using milking machines way back in 1984. Effective and efficient breeding practices led to development of excellent crossbreeds of Holstein Cows. A journey had been from average lactation yield of 2360 liters per cow in 1979 to 5800 liters average lactation yield in 2008. Presently more than 70 cows are in the herd which has recorded a peak yield of 40 liters plus/day. They have established Murrah Buffalo Breeding and Research Center in 2007 and 365 day helpline / call center. Dairy Technology Company management encourages usage of appropriate technology for small, medium, large and mega dairy farms which aims at efficiency, cost effectiveness, and animal welfare along with maximization of farmer’s wealth. Mr. Vaish avers, “commonly used dairy technology are dairy management Software, Cow ids for e.g. pedometers, activity monitors, rumination tags, milking systems, TMR wagons, manure separators and scrapers etc.” Dairy Equipments Equipments helpful in dairy farming are cattle crush, ear tags, brush cutter, reaper, motorized chaff cutter, surgical kit, CMT kit, fat testing equipment, cream separator, hoof trimming kit, fumigator, milk analyzer, spray machine and bio gas plant. “The list is only indicative, and the choice of technology will vary according to farm size, location, available resources and managerial practices,” explains Mr. Vaish. He further adds, “usage of technology should be based on farmers resources, local conditions, farm size i.e. herd strength, choice of breed and after thorough need analysis and farm process mapping, time and motion study of different / target farm activity etc.” Subsidies Available Subsidy on various equipments like milking machine, reaper etc are provided by various state Governments and dairy cooperatives. Also subsidy on bio gas plant is available from central Government. Future Plans Future plans are to set up another 2000 Holstein Crossbreed Breeding Farm by 2015, improving research and development in Murrah Buffalo Breeding and development of dairy clusters and Dairy knowledge centers throughout India. Mr. Vaish shares, “we would also diversify into food business.” Mr. Prateek Vaish holds Masters in Management Sciences. He has rich experience in areas of Strategic HR, retail management & operations, dairy & livestock management, project management etc. He specializes in dairy having strong understanding of farm operation management, system development, process evaluation and audit, mechanization, live stock selection and management, animal logistics and retail management.
