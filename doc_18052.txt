Karnataka Assembly Elections Parliamentary Constituencies Assembly Election Results Election Dates 2013 State Legislative Assembly Karnataka Assembly Elections 2013 Karnataka Elections include the Legislative Assembly Elections, which are held after every five years. The State Election Commission in Karnataka conducts the elections and sees to it that they are carried out in a fair manner. The Karnataka Assembly elections were all set to begin on May 5th, 2013. These elections had played a major role for Congress and BJP. These political parties had roped in their national leaders like Dr. Manmohan Singh, Sonia Gandhi, Rahul Gandhi and Narendra Modi among others to campaign for the polls and get the maximum number of voters via rallies and visits. The polls on 5th May 2013 recorded a 70.23 % voter turnout in the high-stake Karnataka Assembly elections. The polling began at 7 am and the turnout weakened in the afternoon but soon regained momentum in the last few hours. A total of 2,940 candidates were in the fight for the 223 seats in the 224-member House. Despite of Lakhs of voter were left out and got robbed of the right to vote as their names were missing from the electoral list, Karnataka documented the second highest voter turnout in 35 years in the 2013 assembly elections, according to a top electoral office. The exit polls telecast done by various television channels at the end of the voting session, shows that the Congress is likely to win Karnataka and the BJP's maiden rule will end as the party is headed towards a huge defeat. The Karnataka Elections were held for 223 Assembly seats, as one seat of Piriyapatna was postponed to May 28th, 2013 due to the death of the BJP candidate. This time the Indian National Congress (INC) has 224 candidates while the BJP has only 223 candidates. The votes were counted on May 8th, 2013. As the Karnataka election results were declared; BJP suffered a crushing defeat and the Congress won the election gaining power after a gap of seven years. The final results is as following - Congress wins 121 seats, BJP and JDS win 40 seats each, KJP wins 6 seats and other parties won 16 seats.
