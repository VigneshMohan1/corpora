Go to the shop Panchavati Herbals Home Panchavati Herbals Suppliers of raw and processed, organically grown herbs and aromatic oils Srinivas T Panchavati Herbals was established in 2007 at Bangalore as a proprietorship firm by Mr. Srinivas T. It deals in various kinds of herbs, aromatic oils and forestry of Indian origin which include supplies in the form of raw and processed materials and development and research of new practices to grow these herbs to avoid seasonal constraints, thus ensuring regular supply throughout the year. They also source various varieties of famous herbs from around the globe. Their main clients include pharmaceuticals, cosmetics manufacturers, medicinal, agricultural and food industries. Speaking of quality, Mr. Srinivas says that they follow the same practice of Ayurveda at the organization to develop process and grow these herbs, which he says preserves the ancient belief and gives the same beneficial properties. He adds that being established at Bangalore they get the benefit of that rich soil for growth of these herbs which in turn are supervised by professional farmers. He assures that there is no usage of chemicals or other non-natural products in commercialized production thus assuring of the quality. The organization is in the process of implementing the concepts of Fair Trade and Community Trade to avoid exploitation of lab and farmers and to ensure proper wages and dealings. Suppliers of Panchavati Herbals for the range of products are from specialist farms that are exclusive to them, “Thus assuring our customers genuine products at best prices,” shares Mr Srinivas. The company is continuously in touch with local and Government bodies who are renowned worldwide, who conduct various training programs and provide supervision and help for production of herbs and oils. The firm also provides consultancy services with regard to selection of crops suitable to specific geographical region, and provides comprehensive project reports about economic viability, cultivation methods, maintenance, harvesting, post harvesting technology and marketing. They also take up turn-key projects right from planting materials to marketing of the produce. Mr. Srinivas says, “Our charges for these services are nominal and affordable.” Giving suggestions, he notes that there is a need to conserve water through rain water harvesting and drip irrigation as uncertainty of rain is a problem. This would allow a farmer to avail water for free and reduce consumption which in turn allows them to spend less on electricity. He also advises one to be educated about the market trends due to the volatility in market for agriculture commodities. Mr. Srinivas T plans to go for contract and organic farming and expand the business in export market. He is a graduate in Agricultural Science from Bangalore University. He worked for a while in SMPB (State Medicinal Plant Board). While working there he saw the farmers facing problems with regard to right selection of crops and realized the need for proper guidance leading to the establishment of the firm.
