Advanced Search   Latest Photo Essays April 2015 Kyrgyz Republic: Building for Growth The Kyrgyz Republic has undergone an impressive transformation since breaking away from the Soviet Union in 1991. With the support of development partners like ADB, the Central Asian country has made considerable progress in its transition to becoming a market economy. Located at the doorstep of the most economically powerful region in the world and serving as the crossroads between Europe and Asia, the Kyrgyz Republic has a promising future. March 2015 Coloring a World Where Men and Women are Equal As part of an ADB drawing contest, Filipino children put colored pencils to paper to imagine a world where men and women work together in harmony to create a safe future for the next generations. All entries were winners, twelve more so. February 2015 Azerbaijan and ADB: Celebrating 15 Years of Partnership Since Azerbaijan became a member of ADB in 1999, nearly $2 billion has been invested to develop the country’s transport, energy, and water sectors, as well as its small and medium-sized enterprises. January 2015 Water Dam Continues to Raise Farm Yields in Indonesia The Sermo Dam, built in the 1990s in the Indonesian province of Yogyakarta, still provides local farmers and villagers with a reliable supply of water. January 2015 Winds of Change: Pakistan's Zorlu Wind Farm The first privately financed and owned wind farm in Pakistan is now feeding 50 megawatts into the national power-starved grid, drawing more interest to clean, renewable energy projects across the country. January 2015 Improving Water, Power and Health Services in the Marshall Islands Over the last ten years, the quality of life for inhabitants of the Marshall Islands’ Ebeye atoll was greatly improved thanks to a comprehensive health and infrastructure project supported by ADB. December 2014 Asian Development Bank in 2014 It was an eventful year for the Asian Development Bank (ADB) in 2014. Here's a brief recap of some of the major events of the last 12 months.  December 2014 Developing Paradise in the Western Pacific Ocean With ADB support, the Federated States of Micronesia is working to address a series of development issues it faces, including geographic vulnerability, fiscal instability, and a stagnant economy. November 2014 Agribusinesses Rekindle Economic Life in the High Mountains of Nepal A $20 million ADB project is supporting agribusinesses in 10 mountain districts in Nepal, seeking to create 7,500 new jobs by 2017 in one of South Asia's poorest areas. October 2014 Lightening Up the Lives of Low-Income Families in the Marshall Islands A refurbished power generator converted to use biofuel from coconut promises to improve energy supply in the Marshall Islands' atoll of Majuro, lightening up the lives of low-income families.
